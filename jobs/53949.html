  The Position:



As the Field Application Engineer Intern you will be joining the GE Oil & Gas – Measurement & Control team and work on projects aligned to both local and global growth initatives. In this role, you will have an opportunity to work alongside experienced professionals within the field of sales, marketing, engineering, and technology. You will gain exposure supporting both the commercial and engineering teams and assist the greater organization in achieving its annual goals and objectives as an active participant.



Nature of work:



In this role, your responsibilities will include (but are not limited to);



- Work closely with the Customer Innovation Center team on the LNG and Pipeline projects with a focus on the M&C Business leveraging GE initiatives such as the Industrial Internet.



- Gather information about clients' needs and work with the GE team to develop or re-design products that meet customer expectation



- Create product submittals that meet the specifications, circuit plans that exceed customer expectations without error, application logic, and meeting schedule / budget commitments



- Work within the guidelines of applicable GE Policies and Procedures, Engineering Standards, Product Definition Operating Procedures, Specifications, Government and Commercial Standards, and special security requirements



- Manage multiple projects concurrently and ensure quality and schedule requirements are met.



- Effectively interface with field engineers, customers and senior management



- Maintain documentation in the project file to ensure design control requirements are met

 


Job Requirements:

Skills:



- Must be an undergraduate student enrolled in an accredited university pursuing a Bachelor's Degree in Science or Technology



- Must hold and maintain a minimum
3.0 overall or in-major GPA



- Legal authorization to work in Canada is required



Education:



- Bachelor Degree in course of completion in an Engineering or Technology discipline.

 

 
- Excellent interpersonal, written/verbal communication, and leadership skills


- Relevant work experience (prior internships, research, or other experience in software development)


- Passion and aptitude for application engineering with solid technical & analytical skills


- Self-motivated, responsible and disciplined. Detail-oriented and able to meet deadlines


- Basic knowledge of the Microsoft Office suite (Excel, PowerPoint, etc.)


- Ability to apply Six Sigma tools


- Excellent organizational skills

 

Candidates are to submit a cover letter, resume, and transcript. It is a mandatory requirement that you identify your school, cumulative GPA, and anticipated graduation date as part of the application process.

This opportunity is intended for current students who will be returning to their in-class studies upon the completion of the work term. It is not open to candidates who will have graduated prior to the start of this position.

Please note that this position does not provide a subsidy for relocation expenses.
